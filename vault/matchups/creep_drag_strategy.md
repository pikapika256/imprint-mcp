# Mid Lane Strategy -- Creep Dragging

> [2026-05-28]: STRATEGY -- Melee mid heroes can drag creeps behind the enemy tower to counter unfavourable lane matchups. By pulling the creep wave into enemy tower range, the opposing mid laner loses cs and cannot safely fight. This neutralises bad matchups and brings them closer to 50-50.
> [2026-05-28]: WHEN TO USE -- Standard response for melee mids in any matchup outside 40-60 (heavily unfavourable). Examples: Spirit heroes, Tiny, Pangolier, Primal Beast vs Huskar, Viper, Sniper.
> [2026-05-28]: HEROES -- Melee mid heroes generally. Not exclusive to Spirit heroes -- any melee hero that can safely approach the creep wave and drag it has access to this technique.
> [2026-05-28]: DRAFT NOTE -- When drafting a melee mid into a hard counter matchup, the lane is not necessarily lost. Factor in the creep drag option when assessing mid lane viability.

> [2026-05-28]: EFFECTIVENESS TIERS --
> TIER 1 (Sand King, Primal Beast): AOE damage clears the wave behind the tower efficiently, making even heavily unfavourable matchups (e.g. vs Huskar/Viper) closer to 50-50 or potentially better. These two heroes use wave cutting most often and get the most value from it.
> TIER 2 (Earth Spirit, Void Spirit, Tiny, Pangolier): Can execute the wave cut but less effectively. Typically improves matchup to around 40-60 rather than 50-50. Still worth doing in unplayable matchups -- losing 40-60 is far better than losing 20-80.
