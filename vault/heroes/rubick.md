> [2026-05-27]: TIER — Top 5 pos 4 in 7.41c. High priority — steals enemy meta spells and amplifies the Blink/Force Staff item meta.
> [2026-05-27]: Blink Dagger core — enables aggressive spell steal positioning and follow-up on stolen ultimates.
> [2026-05-27]: Force Staff enables repositioning after stealing spells in dangerous positions.
> [2026-05-27]: Extremely high value when enemy team has strong ultimates to steal (Phoenix Supernova, Tidehunter Ravage, Enigma Black Hole).

> [2026-05-27]: PLAYSTYLE REFRAME — In 7.41c Rubick is NOT primarily played as a spell-stealer. His value is as a SAVE SUPPORT. Built-in cast range increase allows him to Force Staff allies from safety, and Aghanims Shard upgrade lets him reposition/save teammates as a core function.
> [2026-05-27]: AGHANIMS SHARD — Key upgrade this patch. Enables Rubick to telekinesis his own allies (reposition as a save), drastically increasing his utility beyond spell stealing.
> [2026-05-27]: FORCE STAFF SYNERGY — Built-in cast range means Rubick can Force Staff allies from further away than other supports, making him an exceptional defensive tool against catch-based enemies.
> [2026-05-27]: STOLEN SPELLS — Secondary benefit, not the primary reason to pick Rubick. Value comes from getting a reliable stun or extra damage — not necessarily game-winning ultimates. Pick Rubick when you need a save support; the spell steal is a bonus.
> [2026-05-27]: DRAFT SIGNAL — If enemy team has strong catch/kill heroes, Rubick is an excellent answer — he negates catches with Force Staff saves AND punishes the enemy by stealing their stun/engage tool.

> [2026-05-28]: FLEX — Genuine pos 4 OR pos 5 this patch. Draft ambiguity is a feature — opponent cannot lock in their lane assignments confidently.
