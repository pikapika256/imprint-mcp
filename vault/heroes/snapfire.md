> [2026-05-28]: POST-LANE ROLE -- High early magic damage. Snowballs well from a strong lane. Can take two distinct item paths depending on the draft and game state.
> [2026-05-28]: ITEM BUILD (SHARED CORE) -- Both paths start with the same core: Boots of Travel, Blink Dagger, Yasha and Kaya, Aghanim's Shard, BKB. This foundation provides mobility, spell amplification, fight access, and spell immunity.
> [2026-05-28]: ITEM BUILD (RIGHT-CLICK PATH) -- After the shared core, build Daedalus and Hydra's Breath. Transitions Snapfire into a physical damage carry. Key advantage: physical damage goes through BKB, allowing continued damage output against enemies who activate spell immunity. A late-game answer to BKB-heavy enemy drafts.
> [2026-05-28]: ITEM BUILD (CASTER PATH) -- After the shared core, build Shiva's Guard, then flexible options from: Octarine Core, Hex, Refresher Orb, Ethereal Blade. Focuses on amplifying and extending the spell damage identity.
> [2026-05-28]: BKB INTERACTION -- Caster path is countered by BKB: once enemies have BKB, magic damage is blocked and Snapfire's spell output is significantly reduced. The right-click path specifically addresses this -- Daedalus + Hydra's Breath provide physical damage that bypasses BKB. Draft read: if the enemy lineup is BKB-heavy, prefer the right-click build path.

> [2026-05-28]: LOCKDOWN -- Snapfire has no reliable lockdown in her base kit. Hex is the standard bridge if the draft requires control, but it is not a core item. Generally built as a high damage core rather than a control hero -- teams drafting Snapfire mid should ensure lockdown is provided elsewhere in the lineup.
