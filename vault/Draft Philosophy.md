# Draft Philosophy

## Lane Structure
> Positions: Mid lane is a solo lane. Side lanes are duo lanes -- position 1 + 5 vs position 3 + 4.

---

## Pick Order Theory

> [2026-05-28]: FIRST PICK ADVANTAGE -- First pick gives first access to meta heroes. The strongest pick slot on first pick is pick 18 -- at this point you have seen 4 enemy heroes and have 2 remaining bans to protect your pick from its worst matchups before the final picks resolve.

> [2026-05-28]: SECOND PICK ADVANTAGE -- Second pick revolves more around counter-picking. The strongest slot is pick 24 -- the last overall pick of the draft. Provides the opportunity to select a hero with full information and no risk of being countered.

> [2026-05-28]: SECOND PICK STRATEGY (FLEX) -- On second pick, taking strong heroes in the first 2 pick slots is valuable. Picking a flex hero early (a hero that can fill multiple roles/positions) preserves optionality. This allows pick 24 to be used for the best available hero given the full draft context, with the flex hero slotting into whatever role is left unfilled.

> [2026-05-28]: SECOND PICK STRATEGY (CORE + BAN) -- When taking a core hero early on second pick, ban the main lane counter to that core immediately after. Removes the primary threat to the lane matchup without sacrificing a ban phase pick.

---

## Lane Concealment

> [2026-05-28]: CONCEALMENT -- Draft with lanes in mind. Concealing which role/lane a core hero will play is beneficial -- forces the opponent to guess and react. When heroes are strong enough the lane becomes less important, but lane matchups still matter in most cases.

> [2026-05-28]: STRONG ENOUGH TO IGNORE LANE -- Some heroes are picked regardless of lane matchup because their strength or the team's ability to play around bad matchups (creep drag, jungling, etc.) outweighs the disadvantage. Alternatively, ban the lane counter directly.

---

## Composition Balance

> [2026-05-28]: TIMING BALANCE -- Cannot draft three late-game scaling cores. The lineup must have at least one hero that is active and creates pressure in the mid game to enable the other cores to reach their item timings. A team of three heroes all spiking at 35+ minutes will lose to a team that fights at 20-25 minutes.

> [2026-05-28]: COMFORT VS FIT -- Heroes are picked based on player comfort AND how they fit the draft. A hero that the player plays well but does not complement the team's win condition is a poor pick. Both criteria must be met.

> [2026-05-28]: SUPPORT STABILITY -- Flex picks are less important for supports. Supports are generally picked early and are relatively stable in their function -- the flex value comes primarily from core heroes. Pick supports for their lane matchup, utility, and synergy rather than worrying about role flexibility.

---

## Meta Pool Strategy

> [2026-05-28]: SECOND PICK META POOL -- If hero pools overlap significantly with the opponent, consider leaving 3 or more of the top meta heroes in the pool rather than banning them. Instead, use bans on second-tier comfort heroes. This guarantees your team will receive at least one top meta hero even if the opponent takes one -- the opponent is forced to pick from the same pool and cannot get all of them.

> [2026-05-28]: FIRST PICK COUNTER STRATEGY -- The counter to the above: on first pick, identify which of the 3+ contested meta heroes counters the others. Take that hero, then ban the third. Forces the second pick team into a difficult position -- they may end up with a hero that is directly countered by what you took.

---

## Meta vs Matchup Analysis

> [2026-05-28]: MATCHUP OVER META -- Meta win rates are not necessarily indicative of how a hero performs against a specific team's style or hero pool. A hero with an 80% win rate in the meta may be weak against your specific lineup or playstyle. Always analyse matchups rather than relying solely on meta statistics.

> [2026-05-28]: TEAM-SPECIFIC READS -- A hero being strong in the general meta does not mean it is strong against you if you play heroes or a style that counters it directly. Weight matchup data higher than raw meta data when assessing threats.

---

## BKB Considerations

> [2026-05-28]: BKB COVERAGE -- When drafting, ensure the lineup has physical damage output and/or BKB-piercing disables to deal with enemy heroes once they purchase BKB. A lineup that is entirely magic damage with no BKB-piercing control becomes significantly weaker as the game progresses.

> [2026-05-28]: KITING AS ALTERNATIVE -- An alternative to countering BKB directly is to draft heroes that can kite BKB users. Heroes with mobility and range can avoid engaging while BKB is active and punish the hero when it expires, rather than trying to fight through it.

---

## Lockdown and Mobility

> [2026-05-28]: MOBILITY COUNTER -- Ensure the draft has enough lockdown to deal with high-mobility heroes (Spirit heroes, Puck, Pangolier, etc.). Lockdown via hero abilities is the most reliable answer.

> [2026-05-28]: ITEMISATION AS FALLBACK -- Lockdown can be addressed through itemisation (Hex, Orchid, Gleipnir) but items take time to complete. The mid game window before cores have these items is a vulnerability -- if the lineup lacks baseline control, it can be exploited in the 15-25 minute window before items come online.
